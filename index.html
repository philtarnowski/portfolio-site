Elate · Founding PM · 2020–2026

0 to 1 Founding PM — Find Product Market Fit

Joined at $20K ARR and <10 customers. With no product function, no analytics baseline, no standard delivery process, and no roadmap, my job was to create and deliver a differentiated product strategy, while building the product org and processes.

→ 100+ customers · multi-million ARR · 200%+ growth consecutive years

The Problem

AI was everywhere, but most B2B SaaS companies were bolting on ChatGPT wrappers and calling it a feature. Elate's customers — strategy and operations leaders — needed something that fit into how they actually worked, not a chatbot in the corner of the UI.

What I Did

I led the product strategy, discovery, and launch of Strategy Advisor — Elate's first standalone AI product, built on Gemini LLMs integrated into core platform workflows. I defined the use cases, designed the interaction model, partnered with engineering on the LLM architecture, and drove GTM alignment on packaging and pricing.

The Outcome

Strategy Advisor drove two of the highest upsell quarters in the company's history, validating AI as a legitimate, monetizable product layer — not just a marketing checkbox.

AI features generate revenue when they're designed around workflow, not around capability. The technical lift was secondary to the product thinking.

The Problem

One of the biggest friction points in Elate's sales cycle was migration. Prospects had strategic plans living in PowerPoints, spreadsheets, and docs — and the work of moving that into Elate was slowing deals and adding implementation cost.

What I Did

I designed and launched Plan Analyzer, an AI feature using structured input parsing and LLM evaluation to analyze a customer's existing strategic plan and map it into Elate's framework. I drove it from discovery through launch — defining the parsing logic, evaluation rubric, and output UX.

The Outcome

Plan Analyzer reduced customer migration time by 40% and directly accelerated upsell velocity. It became a live demo tool in the sales process — something I didn't anticipate but designed toward by keeping the experience clean and fast.

The best AI features solve a problem that exists before the customer even logs in. Reducing time-to-value is a product problem — AI just happened to be the right tool.

The Problem

Elate had strong adoption in plan creation, but customers were leaving the platform to report on progress — going back to slides and spreadsheets for leadership meetings. Lower stickiness, fewer expansion opportunities, a product that only owned half the workflow.

What I Did

I ran 30+ structured customer interviews to understand how strategy leaders actually ran their review meetings. From that, I defined and launched a core reporting module that let customers build, schedule, and share reports without leaving the platform.

The Outcome

Customer interviews are only valuable if you're asking the right questions. I wasn't looking for feature requests — I was mapping the workflow gaps that were costing us retention.

AI Strategy

How I Think About AI

01

This Moment is the Opportunity

Generative AI and the pace of innovation over the past few years has fundamentally changed how we use products, build solutions, and run companies. The companies and people who adapt and innovate now will define the next decade. Standing still isn't a neutral choice.

02

AI Should Solve a Customer Problem — Not Just Apply AI to a Solution

It's easy to bolt an LLM onto a product and call it an AI feature. What's harder — and what actually works — is starting with a real customer problem, understanding the domain deeply, and asking whether AI is genuinely the right tool. The fundamentals of product still apply: know your users, know the market, and build things people will pay for. AI doesn't change that. It just raises the stakes for getting it right.

03

The Best Way to Learn AI Is to Use It

Reading about LLMs and taking courses builds the foundation. But fluency comes from building. I actively use these tools — Claude, ChatGPT, Claude Code, Cowork — to learn design patterns firsthand, understand how the tools actually behave, and develop intuition for what customers will expect from AI-powered products. Every project I build teaches me something I couldn't have learned in a course.
